loscil — Lake Fire

The latest album by loscil, Lake Fire, was released on May 2, 2025, and I finally had the time to listen to it. And also a good reason. Because whose album could be more fitting for the very first post on this blog?

Scott Morgan, aka loscil, was—and still is—a major inspiration. I discovered him around the time of Sea Island, which, for me, set the benchmark for all his later releases. With every new album, I hoped it would sound like this.

Thank God not every album sounded like that. Instead, he evolved and enriched his style without losing what was already great. Lake Fire is no exception. It sounds familiar, yet different. More alive, somehow faster. The sound design is more complex, and at the same time surprisingly melodic (e.g. in “Candling”). It might even be a bit more synth-driven than usual. He once said in an interview that synthesizers were a last resort for him, and that he primarily works with samples (if I remember correctly). But that doesn’t seem to be the case here. The almost signature elements of electronic music—like gating or these rhythmic bass lines—which he has masterfully integrated into ambient music without it sounding overtly electronic, are also more prominent than before (e.g. in “Silos”).

The middle of the album briefly breaks this flow with “Spark” and “Ash Clouds”, which are noticeably softer and slower. “Ash Clouds” in particular really captivated me, and the slightly later track “Doux” carries a beautiful, subtle mood. That creates a transition to the title track “Lake Fire”, which, at over seven minutes and with its extended soundscapes, stands out on the album.
